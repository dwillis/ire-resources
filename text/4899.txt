following resources can help:

U.S. Food and Drug Administration import refusal data – OASIS
Find products that have been refused for entry into the U.S. from your country. Search monthly reports
by product or country here: http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/importrefusals/. An example report
is below – notice the reason for refusal:

These searches are only available by month, but you can also download a CSV file. The CSV files can be
found here: http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/importrefusals/
Please note that the CSV files only list the section of the violation and doesn’t include the detailed
information you find in the monthly reports. You’ll also have to search for your country by a 2-letter ISO
country code. (Google “ISO Code” to find different country codes)

Panjiva

Panjiva is a for-profit service that collects third party trade data from around the world. Use this
database to find information on specific companies, their suppliers as well as individual shipments. The
majority of their information comes from U.S. Customs data that Panjiva has received via FOIA requests.
Search results can include the original shipping bill that has transit details like the name of the vessel,
destination port and more. Panjiva offers free accounts to journalists – just email josh@panjiva.com for
more information.
