Keeping track of trends: (Sites mentioned in ResearchBuzz: http://www.researchbuzz.org/)
• BlogPulse: http://www.blogpulse.com/. Good for searching the blogosphere, but the site
also has an interesting trend tool that allows you to input multiple groups of search terms
and view how the topics correspond to each other over time in a line graph. The results are
based on how often the terms are found in blog posts.
• Internet search trends from Google: http://www.google.com/trends. Google offers a similar
trend graph showing volume of internet searches based on the terms you input. It also
provides bar charts showing results by location on the same page.
• Google Zeitgeist: http://www.google.com/press/zeitgeist.html. This page leads you to the
above site, but also offers a cumulative snapshot of interesting queries people are asking
today.
• FaveBot A Tool for Information Trapping: http://favebot.com/. Allows you to set up your
own search bots to monitor bogs, news, podcasts, and videos. (free, registration required)
• SearchQuilt: http://searchquilt.com/. Yet another source for view what searches are popular.
• InfoSpace SearchSpy: http://www.infospace.com/info.xcite/searchspy. Live search term
viewer.
• ReadBurner, at http://www.readburner.com/. See who's sharing what at Google Reader.
Geography: Often when you are searching for public records in another state, you may have a
city name but need to know the county in which it resides. Here are some good sources for
finding counties and other geographical areas.
• National Association of Counties: http://www.naco.org
Lists counties, county seats, square miles, and the year organized. Click on the county name
to find names and email addresses of county officials, population, and a link to the county’s
web site. You can also sort the county list by population or square miles.

Langenberg.com: http://zip.langenberg.com/. Find zip codes, cities, area codes, counties,
census and time zones, zip code maps. Determine the distance from: city to zip or zip to city,
or discover surrounding zip codes.
ZipInfo: http://zipinfo.com/search/zipcode.htm
Entering zip code, city, or city and state yields: County name, FIPS code, Time zone,
MSA/PMSA, Area code and Latitude/longitude.
