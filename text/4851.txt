Part I: The Audience

Part 1: The audience
Dear	  Newsrooms	  of	  America:	  
We	  are	  writing	  a	  letter	  
because	  we	  didn’t	  think	  this	  
would	  all	  fit	  in	  your	  comments	  
section — and	  we	  know	  that	  
you	  don’t	  often	  read	  the	  
comments	  anyway.
We	  want	  to	  know:	  As	  your	  
customers,	  why	  don’t	  you	  
seem	  to	  care	  about	  us?	  Why	  
do	  think	  you	  know	  what’s	  best	  
for	  us	  without	  even	  asking	  us?

Part II: The management
For	  too	  long	  we’ve	  assumed	  the	  position	  of	  
experts,	  as	  knowing	  what’s	  best,	  and	  exerting	  
our	  power	  rather	  than	  sharing	  it	  with	  those	  
we	  serve.
The	  writing	  is	  on	  the	  wall:	  engaging	  
audiences	  is	  no	  longer	  a	  nice	  thing	  to	  do,	  but	  
essential	  for	  our	  survival.	  It	  is	  our	  job	  to	  
prove	  to	  our	  communities	  that	  what	  we	  do	  is	  
of	  value.	  Working	  with	  our	  communities,	  
while	  being	  transparent	  about	  our	  process	  
and	  our	  struggles,	  is	  the	  most	  direct	  way	  to	  
show	  them	  our	  value.

Part III: The newsroom
At	  the	  core	  of	  our	  resistance	  is	  a	  tacit	  
assumption	  that	  engagement	  isn’t	  
journalism.	  What	  do	  you	  mean	  “collaborate	  
with	  the	  public”?	  What	  do	  you	  mean	  “let	  
people	  tell	  their	  own	  stories”?	  That’s	  not	  
what	  we	  do.
Yet	  if	  we’re	  honest	  with	  ourselves,	  we	  
realize	  that	  we	  constantly	  rely	  on	  
information	  from	  third	  party	  people	  that	  we	  
deem	  authoritative,	  but	  somehow	  we	  
demean	  input	  from	  “the	  public.”	  Perhaps	  
that’s	  part	  of	  the	  reason	  our	  audience	  isn’t	  
as	  diverse	  as	  we	  know	  it	  should	  be,	  and	  why	  
our	  work	  isn’t	  relevant	  to	  many	  people.

First	  steps?	  
• Emphasize	  the	  ways engagement	  is	  really	  part	  of	  reporting — that	  it’s	  central	  
to	  our	  work:	  listening,	  seeking	  out	  sources,	  finding	  leads,	  seeking	  out	  
information,	  reporting	  back.
• Come	  up	  with	  a	  specific	  project — or	  an	  existing	  story	  or	  beat — that	  would	  
be	  well	  suited	  to	  engagement.
• Identify	  people	  in	  the	  newsroom	  who	  are	  interested	  and	  passionate,	  with	  an	  
entrepreneurial	  and	  experimental	  spirit,	  who	  are	  up	  for	  this	  work	  and	  willing	  to	  
try	  it.
