Health
1) CADRIS: a database that tracks adverse drug reactions.
2) NOC: a database that stores all the drugs which have given a “notice of compliance,” or a green
light to go to market. Text files can also be downloaded from Health Canada’s web site at:
http://www.hc-sc.gc.ca/dhp-mps/prodpharma/notices avis/list/index_e.html#Weekly%20Updates
3) Drug products: All the drugs that are on the market after they have obtained a notice of
compliance. You can download the files by going online at: http://www.hc-sc.gc.ca/dhpmps/prodpharma/databasdon/dpd_bdpp_data_extract_e.html
4) Medical devices: All adverse reactions involving medical devices. It’s available through the
federal access to information law.
5) Recalled products: a list on Health Canada’s web site at: http://209.217.71.106/PR/homeaccueil-e.jsp
6) FDA
7) WHO

Transportation
1) CADORS: Transport Canada’s database that keeps track of plane accidents, near misses and
other events causing a disruption in passenger service or danger to passengers. You can search
online at: http://www.tc.gc.ca/aviation/applications/cadors/English/Query/queryframe.asp
2) Fines: Companies fined can be found online at:
http://www.tc.gc.ca/CivilAviation/standards/Enforcement/Publications/Corporate/summary.hm
3) The Transportation Safety Board of Canada: The board keeps monthly statistics for pipeline,
airline, rail and marine accidents, fatal and otherwise. The tables contain aggregate stats. The

recent ones from 2006 and 2007 can be downloaded into Excel. Stats that go farther back than
that are contained in annual reports in pdf format. However, you can ask the board to send you
the stats from earlier years in database format. To see the stats, please go to:
http://www.tsb.gc.ca/en/stats/index.asp?section=3.1
4) Recalled cars: http://www.tc.gc.ca/roadsafety/recalls/search_e.asp

Canadian Border Services Agency
1) Selected Commodities Seizure Report: It’s only available in tiff format, though the CBC has a
complaint with the federal Information Commissioner to obtain the entire database.
2) Arms trade data: This data is obtained from Statistics Canada and the U.S. Census Bureau (U.S.
Department of Commerce. The data can also be located online at:
http://strategis.ic.gc.ca/sc_mrkti/tdst/tdo/tdo.php#tag In order to obtain seven year’s worth of
export and import data, you can contact Statistics Canada. Within days, the agency can send you
the material in two separate spreadsheets.

The Royal Canadian Mounted Police
1) Canada's National Gun Registry: The Ottawa Citizen used the database for its series, Rapid Fire,
and then put a searchable version of the database online at:
http://www.canada.com/ottawacitizen/features/rapidfire/form.html. The database can be
obtained using the federal access to information act.

Canada Revenue Agency
1) Charities registration: Database can be obtained through the federal access to information law.
2) Charities: The website contains a searchable database of all registered charities. The database
can also be downloaded: http://www.cra-arc.gc.ca/tax/charities/online_listings/canreg_interime.html

Environment Canada
1) National Pollutant Release Inventory: They can be downloaded by visiting:
http://www.ec.gc.ca/pdb/npri/npri_dat_rep_e.cfm#databases
2) Greenhouse emissions: data can be downloaded at:
http://www.ec.gc.ca/pdb/ghg/onlinedata/DataAndReports_e.cfm

The Treasury Board Secretariat
1) Proactive disclosures: By law, all government departments have to reveal contracts that are more than
10-thousand dollars. These revelations are called “proactive disclosures.” Each government department
has a proactive disclosure tab at the bottom left-hand side of the page in the menu section. For a
complete list of departments with these disclosures , please go to: http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pddp/index_e.asp

Access to Information

1) CAIRS: This is an online database maintained by yours truly that tracks all access-to-information requests
made to federal government, departments, agencies, boards and Crown corporations, etc. You can find
CAIRS at: http://www.onlinedemocracy.ca/CAIRS/CAIA-OD.htm
