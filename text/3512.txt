Emerging Trends in State Public Records Cases and Legislation:

1. efforts to close 911 tapes from public inspection or use (Ga.'s S.B.
98):http://www.ncga.state.nc.us/Sessions/2011/Bills/Senate/PDF/S98v1.pdf &
pending in Tennessee:
http://wapp.capitol.tn.gov/apps/BillInfo/Default.aspx?BillNumber=HB1539

2. Florida law closes crime scene death photos/videos:
http://static.lobbytools.com/bills/2011/PDF/0411ER.PDF

3. Texas law closes photographs and IA files of police officers, with limited
exceptions (H.B. 2006): http://e-lobbyist.com/gaits/text/290607
See also (re: access to IA files): Romero v. City of Fountain (Colo. App. 2011) &
http://www.rcfp.org/newsitems/index.php?i=11890

4. Continued battles over access to public officials' appointment calendars, telephone logs,
e-mails, etc.:
http://www.mercurynews.com/rss/ci_18211066?source=rss&nclick_check=1
http://www.denverpost.com/news/ci_17962571
http://www.examiner.com/hispanic-community-in-miami/herald-to-obtain-mayor-s-phone-recordsfollowing-lawsuit-settlement

5. Efforts to put public employees' dates of birth out of reach, to protect against identity
theft. (rejected in Texas).
Compare: http://www.supreme.courts.state.tx.us/historical/2010/dec/080172.pdf &
http://openrecords.state.pa.us/portal/server.pt/document/1021130/20100845_purcelloa_pdf_(3)
Pending: http://newsok.com/sunshine-week-state-employee-birth-date-issue-in-limbo-asoklahoma-supreme-court-considers-appeals/article/3549328

II.

Recent Developments Under the Federal FOIA:

1. Supreme Court's rejection of a right to “personal privacy” for corporations:
http://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/10pdf/09-1279.pdf

2. federal court’s ruling that metadata must be produced in response to a FOIA request:
http://proteggallc.files.wordpress.com/2011/02/ndlo-vs-ice-metadata-fields-productionscheindlin.pdf
(as have several state courts, see O’Neill v. City of Shoreline (Wash. 2010):
http://www.rcfp.org/newsitems/docs/20101008_151627_shoreline_opinion.pdf &
http://www.steptoe.com/assets/attachments/4095.pdf (surveying state court rulings)).

3. federal appeals court upholds Bureau of Prisons’ withholding of a security video
showing a prisoner’s murder, despite that it was played in open court:
http://ca10.washburnlaw.edu/cases/2011/01/09-1511.pdf
