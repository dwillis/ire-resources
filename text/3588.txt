Spycraft: Keeping your
sources private
Steve Doig
Cronkite School of Journalism
Arizona State University

Why spycraft for reporters?
z Need to keep identity of confidential

sources secret from subpoena.
z Need to keep identity of confidential
whistleblowers secret from corporations.
z Need to travel in places where
governments detain journalists.

What I’ll cover
z Keeping internet searches private
z Making and receiving untraceable calls
z Keeping email private
z Encryption/decryption programs
z Keeping your computer clean
z Tricking keyloggers

Private internet searching
z AOL debacle: 36 million search terms of

650,000 users (http://www.aolstalker.com/)
z Subpoenas to your IT department or IP
provider
z Alternative: www.ixquick.com: No IP
addresses kept, search terms deleted within
48 hours
z Anonymizer.com: Anonymous Surfing ($30)
z Privoxy.org: Web proxy that filters headers

Keeping identity private in calls
z *67 blocks Caller ID
z “Spoof” your Caller ID with SpoofCard

(www.spoofcard.com) or other spoofing
services -- $10/60 minutes
z Spoofcard also does voice changing

Cellphone cautions
z GIS-equipped cellphones track your

location
z Cellphones also track location by cell
tower triangulation
z Cellphones and wireless phones can be
heard by scanners
z Cellphones can be bugged

Cellphone spyware
z Listen to calls, extract SMS, view photos,

read call logs ($100)

Spy cameras

Pre-paid cell phones
z No-contract cell phones: Buy with cash,

and replenish with cash
z Various providers: AT&T, Verizon, TMobile, Virgin Mobile
z Phones as cheap as $10-$20 at
Walmart, Best Buy
z Pre-paid cards as cheap as 10
cents/minute

Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP)
z Internet voice calls
z Beware “man in the middle” attacks
z Skype encrypts voice/video data stream
z But there may be a back door…

z Zfone with VoIP clients like Gizmo,

GoogleTalk, Magic Jack

Keeping identity private in email
z Use free “throwaway” email addresses

from Yahoo, etc.
z Anonymizer.com: Nyms software creates
throwaway email addresses that will
forward to your real address
z Anonymous.org: Free web-based
remailer

TorProject.org
z Network run by privacy activists,

originally developed by U.S. Navy
z Uses “onion routing” to pass packets
through randomly-selected nodes
z Combine with Privoxy

z Use micro SD cards
z 2GB

Smuggling text and pictures

Cryptography
z Avoid simple ciphers, one-time pads, etc.
z Public-key cryptography is best
z Pretty Good Privacy: www.pgp.com
z Email encryption
z Disk encryption
z Instant-message encryption
z Desktop Home edition: $99

z Cryptoheaven.com
z Freeware PGP available
z Keep data on encrypted thumb drive

(Folderlock)

Steganography
z Poe’s “Purloined Letter”: Hide in plain sight
z Message hidden in “covertext” of some sort:
z Plaintext
z MP3s, jpegs, video, etc.

z Message can be text, picture, audio,

video
z Steganos Privacy Suite 12: $70
z stegoarchive.com for shareware programs

Watermarking, fingerprinting
z Related to steganography
z Hidden information embedded in files
z Printer steganography: serial #, date, time
z Invisible watermarking uses variety of

techniques: Shift lines, text and/or characters;
deliberate misspellings, etc.
z Used to verify copyright, reveal image
tampering, traitor tracing
z Strategy: Retype the document, adding your
own variations…

Cleaning your computer
z Deleting files doesn’t destroy them
z Need software that overwrites deleted

file space, temp files, etc.
z Webroot Window Washer: $30
z Deletes whatever you specify
z “Bleaches”: Overwrites 3, 7 or 35 times
z Can schedule washes as often as every 15

minutes

Keyloggers
z Hidden program that captures

keystrokes and sends them to whoever
installed it.
z FBI’s Magic Lantern keylogger
z Anti-spyware software will detect many –
but not all – keyloggers.
z Stopgap protection: When typing
password letters, type a few random
letters elsewhere on window between
each

Hardware keyloggers
z Insert between keyboard and computer

($50-$200)
