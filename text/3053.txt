The Bupe Fix revealed how a drug the federal government promotes for treating
narcotics addicts can cause some of the same problems it was intended to curb. While
buprenorphine, which is sold mainly under the brand name Suboxone, has proven
effective for many patients, abuse is growing. Here are a few sites:
http://buprenorphine.samhsa.gov

The Substance Abuse Mental Health Services Administration’s Center for Substance
Abuse Treatment can send Excel spreadsheets on use of the drug and some doctors who
prescribe it, by state, as well as guidelines for prescribing it.
http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/arcos/retail_drug_summary/index.html

The Drug Enforcement Administration ARCOS system track sales of controlled
pharmaceuticals by state.
http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cder/drugsatfda/

U.S. Food and Drug Administration site that has details on drug approvals as well as
links to many documents submitted by drug makers to gain approval.
http://www.aapc.org/

Poison control centers in every state keep statistics on reported abuse for many drugs.
This site has links to all of the centers.
http://www.natlalliance.org/prescription_drug.asp

This group advocates for precription monitoring programs to limit diversion. Many pain
patients and others oppose these systems on privacy grounds.
http://www.naabt.org/

The National Alliance of Advocates for Buprenorphine Treatment, which accepts grants
from the drug’s manufacturer, has a nice archive of documents on the drug and sponsors
patient discussion boards.
http://forum.opiophile.org/

One of several lively discussion boards where users of narcotic drugs share their views
not only on drugs various abuse treatments.
http://www.suboxone.com/

This is the official site for Suboxone manufacturer Reckitt Benckiser Pharmaceuticals.
The company also helps sponsor some other sites.
