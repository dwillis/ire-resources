INFORMATION RESOURCES FOR U.S. – MEXICO CRIME &
JUSTICE
The following is a list of selected online resources for research
and current awareness of U.S.-Mexico crime and justice issues
Compiled by Molly Molloy, New Mexico State University Library, mmolloy@nmsu.edu
Updated March 16, 2005

Official Sources: United States
United States Department of Justice
http://www.usdoj.gov/
DOJ is the main executive department for law enforcement in the United
States. Mission statement of the Department of Justice: To enforce the law
and defend the interests of the United States according to the law; to ensure
public safety against threats foreign and domestic; to provide Federal
leadership in preventing and controlling crime; to seek just punishment for
those guilty of unlawful behavior; to administer and enforce the Nation's
immigration laws fairly and effectively; and to ensure fair and impartial
administration of justice for all Americans.
The following agencies are part of the DOJ and publish many reports and
statistics online:
National Criminal Justice Reference Service
http://www.ncjrs.org/
Part of the Department of Justice, Office of Justice Programs, the
“outreach” agency of the DOJ. NCJRS provides many online documents
and databases of criminal justice information. From the website:
“NCJRS is a federally funded resource offering justice and substance
abuse information to support research, policy, and program
development worldwide.”
Bureau of Justice Statistics
http://www.ojp.usdoj.gov/bjs/
The BJS collects, analyzes, publishes, and disseminates information on
crime, criminal offenders, victims of crime, and the operation of justice
systems at all levels of government. A primary source for crime
statistics compiled by various government agencies.
Drug Enforcement Administration
http://www.usdoj.gov/dea/index.htm
The mission of the DEA is to enforce the controlled substances laws
and regulations of the United States and bring to the criminal and civil
justice system of the United States, or any other competent
jurisdiction, those organizations and principal members of
organizations, involved in the growing, manufacture, or distribution of
controlled substances appearing in or destined for illicit traffic in the
United States; and to recommend and support non-enforcement

programs aimed at reducing the availability of illicit controlled
substances on the domestic and international markets.
Federal Bureau of Investigation
http://www.fbi.gov/
The FBI is a major source for statistics on crime and justice in the US.
See http://www.fbi.gov/publications.htm for a listing and links to FBI
publications, including the major source for US crime statistics, the
Uniform Crime Reports, Crime in the United States,
http://www.fbi.gov/ucr/ucr.htm#cius
United States Department of Homeland Security
http://www.dhs.gov/
Investigative and enforcement responsibilities for enforcement of federal
immigration laws, customs laws, and air security laws was transferred to the
U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). For more information on
what ICE is doing, please check the ICE Web site. U.S. Customs and Border
Protection (CBP) assumed responsibilities for protecting our borders within
the Department of Homeland Security. For more information about CBP and
its activities, please check the CBP Web site.

Advocacy & News Resources

Amigos de las Mujeres de Júarez
http://www.amigosdemujeres.org/
Works toward ending crimes against women in Ciudad Juárez and Chihuahua
City and provide financial, logistical, and emotional support to the families of
the Cd. Juárez and Chihuahua City serial-killing victims and the Casa Amiga
rape and abuse crisis center in Cd. Juárez.
Casa Amiga—Centro de Crisis
http://www.casaamigajuarez.org/
Casa Amiga provides counseling, legal and medical services as needed to the
victims of domestic abuse, sexual abuse and rape. Director Esther Chavez
Cano has been recognized around the world numerous times for her work on
Cd. Juárez women's issues.
Amnesty International, Stop Violence Against Women in Ciudad
Juárez and Chihuahua, México
http://www.amnestyusa.org/women/juarez/
Access to important documentation prepared by this international advocacy
organization.

Washington Office on Latin America
http://www.wola.org/
WOLA monitors the impact of U.S. foreign policy on human rights, democracy
and equitable development in Latin America. WOLA informs and educates
policy-makers, religious and non-governmental organizations, and the general
public about that impact. In addition, WOLA’s work on Mexico has focused on
drug policy and violence. See for example:
http://www.wola.org/publications/publications.htm#DrugPolicy and
http://www.wola.org/Mexico/mexico.htm
En Linea Directa
http://www.enlineadirecta.info/
News site from Tamaulipas, created by journalist Arturo Solis. Excellent

Al Margen
http://www.almargen.com.mx/
Investigative reporting, documentary photography, media and literary
criticism from a team of reporters, photojournalists and other writers based in
Ciudad Júarez, Chihuahua.
Hemeroteca Digital de Chihuahua
http://www.inpro.com.mx/english/index2.html
The Hemeroteca Digital de Chihuahua is a subscription database provided by
Información Procesada (INPRO). It contains full text articles from Mexican
newspapers and magazines from and about the state of Chihuahua and the
U.S.-Mexico border region. Time coverage: 1976 to the present. For more
information about access to this database, email: informes@inpro.com.mx.
Google Scholar
http://scholar.google.com/

Google Scholar enables the search for scholarly literature, including peerreviewed papers, theses, books, preprints, abstracts and technical reports
from all areas of research. Some articles are freely available—usually those
from a non-profit organization or government agency; articles published in
academic journals may be available for purchase from an online document
delivery service. A search on the keywords “mexico crime” yields more
than 8,000 citations.
Google Alerts
http://www.google.com/alerts

Google Alerts are emails automatically sent to you when there are new
Google results for your search terms from Google News sources and from the
web. This is a way to follow news stories and receive updates whenever a
new article appears.
