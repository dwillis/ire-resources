Here are some things to know about banking data:
Overview of U.S. banking

• 8,300 banks v. 11,000 in 1992
• Deposits = $9 trillion
• Loans = $7.88 trillion
• Profits = $16 billion in 2008 ($100 billion a year ago)

Key bank numbers
• Assets

• Interest income

– Loans (minus loss allowance)

– Primarily on loans

• Interest Expense

– Securities
– OREO

– Primarily on deposits

• Provision for loan losses
• Non-interest income

– Goodwill

• Liabilities
– Primarily deposits

– Fees

• Capital

– Securities sales

Getting bank data
• Each bank reports quarterly to FDIC (“call report” or “condition statement”)
• Reports are public
• Highly detailed (a 50-page PDF)
