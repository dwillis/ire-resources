Tip sheet: Investigating the immigration crisis on the border
Valeria Fernández and Jude Joffe-Block
IRE, Sept 23, 2020
Reaching immigrants in detention
-Phoning people in detention:
-Gettingout App (available at some facilities) https://www.gettingout.com/
It’s been used by detainees to send videos to the media through family members. See:
https://theintercept.com/2020/04/28/ice-detention-coronavirus-videos/
Fact-checking resources
For detained immigrants:
Collecting A numbers
Using media waivers. (They are available both in English and Spanish)
https://www.ice.gov/doclib/news/library/forms/pdf/60-001.pdf
All cases: FOIA’s for immigration files. Use form G-639 for USCIS (If they are alive they have to
sign a waiver, if the person is deceased you need a death certificate.)
https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/g-639.pdf
Sometimes the attorney will have this. Also try to get file from ICE and court documents for the
Executive Office of Immigration Review (EOIR).
