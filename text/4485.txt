TIPSHEET
PANEL: How to sort out science for your investigation
TIME: Thursday, June 4, 2015
PANELIST: Jeff Donn
Here are several places to find scientific research and sources and to vet sources.

RESEARCH
PubMed search interface for biomedical literature, including some that link to full text
http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed
PLOS search tool for Public Library of Science
https://www.plos.org/
ScienceDirect publisher Elsevier's search tool, including some free articles
http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/search
Google Scholar search tool for scholarly articles and books, including some that are free
http://scholar.google.com/
EXPERTS
EurekAlert! directory of public information officers at research institutions.
http://sciencesources.eurekalert.org/
University of York register of systematic reviews allows you to identify experts doing current research in
a field
http://www.crd.york.ac.uk/PROSPERO/
The Integrity in Science Database to find conflicts of interest
http://www.cspinet.org/integrity/
