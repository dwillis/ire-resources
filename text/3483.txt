State and local economic data
Moody’s Analytics: 70+ economic variables by state, metro area and county; employment and
output; consumer credit volume; foreclosures [www.economy.com]
Federal Reserve regional banks: 12 across U.S., each with a team of regional economists and
directors who run companies [www.dallasfed.org/data/resources.html]
Bureau of Economic Analysis and Bureau of Labor Statistics: GDP, employment, inflation by state or
metro area; also have regional economists [www.bea.gov/regional/index.htm and
www.bls.gov/eag/eag.us.htm]
Bank economists: good low‐level data [www.wellsfargo.com/com/research/economics]

Municipal finance
Moody’s and S&P: the two giants of the municipal‐bond rating world, with armies of analysts and
research down to local level [www.moodys.com/researchandratings and
www.standardandpoors.com/ratings/govs‐uspf/en/us]
Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board: the SEC of municipal bonds; filings include offering
documents (with Wall Street underwriter), recent trades, unscheduled draws on reserves; detailed
searching [emma.msrb.org/Search/Search.aspx?hlt=search]
Pew Center on the States: research on the fiscal peril facing U.S. states, pensions and retiree health
benefits [www.pewcenteronthestates.org]

Pensions and 401(k) plans
U.S. Department of Labor: Form 5500 shows by how much individual pension plans are
underfunded; annual filings also include 401(k) plans
[www.efast.dol.gov/portal/app/disseminate?execution=e2s1]
Pension Benefit Guaranty Corp.: details about pension funds now under U.S. control
[www.pbgc.gov/res/search.html]
Pension‐plan holdings: gains and losses on investments; assumptions on rates of return; firms that
get investment business from the pension plan
[www.calpers.ca.gov/index.jsp?bc=/investments/reports/home.xml]

Companies
Securities and Exchange Commission: set up alerts to get company filings in real time
[secfilings.com/MyAlerts.aspx and www.investorscopes.com]

Business courts: some states have specialized courts that handle business disputes
[www.ncbusinesscourt.net]
Pacer: U.S. district court and bankruptcy‐court filings; costs 8 cents per page, or free if monthly
total is less than $10 [www.pacer.gov]

Financial crisis
Availability of credit: Lender‐by‐lender “call reports” show loans outstanding in great detail, with
limited disclosure of new loans; filed quarterly by all U.S. banks, savings institutions and credit
unions [www2.fdic.gov/call_tfr_rpts/ and
cuonline.ncua.gov/CreditUnionOnline/CU/FindCreditUnions.aspx]
Dead banks: U.S. regulators conduct autopsies of failed banks and savings institutions, typically
with new details about their disastrous mistakes [www.fdicoig.gov]
