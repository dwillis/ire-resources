Internet sites on health care fraud and abuse. Many have links to other useful sites:
http://www.oig.hhs.gov/ U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office of
Inspector General. This site lists hundreds of reports on Medicare and Medicaid fraud
from ambulance rip-offs to X-rays.
http://www.gao.gov U.S. General Accounting Office, the audit arm of Congress, has
hundreds of audit reports with a search engine by subject.
http://defaulteddocs.dhhs.gov/cgi-bin/ddocs_counter.pl HHS Health Resources and
Services Administration lists $166,710,667 in student loan defaults by doctors, from
chiropractors to veterinarians. Can search by name.
http://www.healthlaw.org: This is the site of the National Health Law Project, an
advocacy group for the poor that does a good job explaining complex changes in
Medicaid and offering its views on them.
http://exclusions.oig.hhs.gov/ A very large health site with a great section on fraud and
abuse that lists numerous articles and reports on fraud.
http://www.fsmb.org/members.htm Federation of State Medical Boards list of links to
state licensing boards. Many can be searched to find disciplinary actions against a doctor.
http://naag.org/ National Association of Attorneys General site, with links to each state
and some news on scams and fraud in health care.
http://www.bizjournals.com/search.html Business journals in more than 40 cities can be
searched for health care news and fraud to spot trends.
http://www.va.gov/oig/search.htm - search Site for searching for audits of care in
veterans hospitals across the country.
http://hippo.findlaw.com/hippohome.html Hippo is a chatty site that has good links to a
wide variety of health care policy information.
http://www.medicare.gov Site for the U.S. Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services,
with quality ratings for nursing homes and home health care agencies nationwide.
